# Government & Institutional Sustainability Benchmarks

## 1. National Green Building Standards & Rating Systems
* Green building ratings (such as LEED, GRIHA in India, or equivalent national standards) establish criteria for evaluating educational institutions.
* Key parameters for rating points:
  * Sustainable site planning (maintaining natural soil permeability, preserving mature trees).
  * Water efficiency (reducing indoor potable water usage by 30% or more compared to a baseline).
  * Energy efficiency (demonstrating savings of 10-30% above ASHRAE energy performance standards).
  * Building materials (use of recycled and local materials).
  * Indoor environmental quality (natural ventilation, daylight factor compliance).

## 2. Institutional Resource Benchmarks (National Averages)
* **Electricity Consumption:**
  * Baseline academic block standard: 20-30 kWh per student per year.
  * Green campus standard: Less than 15 kWh per student per year.
* **Water Consumption:**
  * Baseline student residential usage: 135 liters per capita per day (LPCD) including flushing, bathing, washing, and dining.
  * Water-efficient campus target: Less than 90 LPCD through low-flow fixtures and recycling.
* **Carbon Emissions Intensity:**
  * National electricity grid factor: Approximately 0.82 to 0.85 kg CO2 emitted per kWh consumed.
  * Direct emissions baseline: 1.5 metric tons CO2 equivalent per student per year.
  * Net-zero pathway target: Reducing grid consumption by 50% via energy savings and solar offsetting.

## 3. Policy Directives on Solar Power Integration
* State policies often mandate that educational canteens, hostels, and academic institutions with a connected load of over 100 kW must install solar PV systems covering at least 10% of their electricity load capacity.
* Net metering connections must be approved by the state electricity board within 45 days of application, enabling financial viability.
